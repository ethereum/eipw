markdown-requires-ref-body

Proposals listed in the requires header must be referenced somewhere in the body.

Examples

error[markdown-requires-ref-body]: proposals listed in preamble header `requires` must be referenced in the body
  |
2 | requires: 4, 5, 6
  |              ^ not referenced in body
  |

Explanation

The markdown-requires-ref-body lint is the counterpart to the preamble-requires-ref-... family. Together they keep the requires header and the body in sync: the preamble lints catch proposals mentioned in title or description that were forgotten in requires, while this lint catches proposals listed in requires that are never explained in the body.

References inside fenced code blocks, inline code, and HTML are not counted, since those are usually illustrative and not a real dependency on the proposal.
